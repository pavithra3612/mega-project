## Crime and Incarceration Analysis - New Mexico Focus

---

## üéØ **MAIN FINDING: The New Mexico Paradox**

**New Mexico has the HIGHEST crime rate in the nation (2016) but only average incarceration rates.**

This creates a **disconnect** between crime levels and incarceration policies - a perfect topic for your "Access to Justice" presentation!

---

## üìä **Slide 1: The Shocking Statistic**

- **New Mexico ranked #1 in crime rate (2016)** - Highest in the entire United States
  - Crime Rate: 4,628 per 100,000 population
  - National Average: 3,409 per 100,000
  - **32.9% HIGHER than national average**

- **But incarceration rate is only #30** - Middle of the pack
  - Incarceration Rate: 335 per 100,000
  - National Average: 396 per 100,000
  - **17.2% LOWER than national average**

**Key Question:** Why does the state with the most crime have relatively low incarceration rates?

---

## üìà **Slide 2: Trends Over Time (2001-2016)**

### Crime Rate Trend:
- **2001:** 5,319 per 100k
- **2016:** 4,628 per 100k
- **Change: -13.0%** (Crime is DECREASING)

### Incarceration Rate Trend:
- **2001:** 310 per 100k
- **2016:** 335 per 100k
- **Change: +8.4%** (Incarceration is INCREASING)

**The Paradox:** Crime went DOWN 13%, but incarceration went UP 8.4%!

This is the OPPOSITE of what you'd expect if incarceration was directly responding to crime.

---

## üîç **Slide 3: The Divergent Trend Pattern**

**19 states show the same pattern:** Crime decreased but incarceration increased

Examples:
- **Arizona:** Crime DOWN 42.9%, Incarceration UP 17.1%
- **Arkansas:** Crime DOWN 7.1%, Incarceration UP 24.3%
- **Florida:** Crime DOWN 44.2%, Incarceration UP 7.3%

**This suggests systemic issues beyond just responding to crime levels.**

---

## üìâ **Slide 4: Correlation Analysis**

**Overall Correlation: 0.432** (Moderate correlation)

- This means crime and incarceration are related, but NOT strongly
- Many factors beyond crime rates influence incarceration
- **Policy, resources, and justice system efficiency matter more than crime alone**

## üìä **Slide 6: Incarceration-to-Crime Ratio**

**New Mexico Ratio: 0.072** (Rank: #47 out of 50)

This means: **For every 1,000 crimes, only 72 people are incarcerated**

**States with highest ratios (most incarceration per crime):**
1. Idaho: 0.217 (217 per 1,000 crimes)
2. Connecticut: 0.206
3. Delaware: 0.203

**New Mexico incarcerates at less than HALF the rate of high-ratio states, despite having the highest crime rate.**

### 1. **The Efficiency Question**
